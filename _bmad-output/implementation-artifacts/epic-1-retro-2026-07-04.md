## 2. What Went Well

- **The cross-story learning loop was the standout of the epic** (confirmed by Borja as the single most valuable outcome). Each story carried a *"Previous story intelligence"* section that inherited learnings: 1.2 started from 1.1's completion notes; 1.3 from 1.2's notes plus `deferred-work.md`. Scaffold-stage mistakes were not repeated. This is the most reusable asset the epic produced.
- **Hard spine decisions were resolved early and documented with rationale.** The source-exports pattern in 1.1 — exporting `@hivly/shared` types directly from raw `.ts` instead of TypeScript project references — sidestepped the `composite: true` vs `noEmit` conflict and supported all three stories without friction.
- **"Done" meant verified against reality, not just compiled.** Story 1.3 was validated against a live stack: `docker compose config` returned exactly 7 services and the migrator applied all 8 tables.

## 3. Challenges

- **Production-hardening debt is flagged in every review and consistently deferred.** SIGTERM/SIGINT were flagged in the 1.1 review and closed by 1.3 (now present in all three services: `bot`, `workers`, `backend`). What remains is `uncaughtException`/`unhandledRejection` handlers (absent in all three) and *real* graceful shutdown for `bot`/`workers` once they hold real connections. Assessed as **linear, not exponential, debt** — does not block Epic 2 (no SSE or Redis consumers in flight yet).
- **Specs diverged from reality in fine detail, discovered only during implementation.** 1.2: AC said 7 tables, data-model needed 8 (`user_roles_cache`). 1.3: AC required "exactly 7 services" but the original `webdist`-volume design needed an 8th one-shot service — revised to SPA baked into the nginx image (Borja-approved 2026-07-04). Both resolved correctly within the story and docs updated. Assessed as a **non-problem with a caveat**: run a quick AC-consistency pass before creating the Epic 2 stories.
- **Test coverage is unit-only** — the most consequential gap for Epic 2, which introduces OAuth2 flows, Discord callbacks, and RBAC: exactly the surface that unit tests mock rather than validate.

## 4. Concrete Blocker for Epic 2

`packages/web/src/main.tsx` imports the full `@hivly/shared` root barrel, which re-exports the `db` module (`pg`), so Vite pulls `pg` + Node built-ins into the browser bundle (~408 KB, harmless at placeholder scope). Epic 2 Story 2.1 (design system in `packages/web`) is the first place this can bite. Subpath exports already make the fix easy (`@hivly/shared/schemas`), but web imports must be audited **before 2.1**.
